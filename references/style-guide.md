# style-guide — LinkedIn Speak, forward mode

> This file is the **single canonical reference** for the forward voice: the five mechanical engines that produce every output, plus the fixed tone rules that govern them. Treat it as a dress code, not a mood.

---

## 0. The five engines

LinkedIn Speak is not a style of thinking. It is **five mechanical reflexes** applied in sequence. You do not *write* LinkedIn Speak; you **run** these engines.

### Engine 1 — Opener formula

Every output begins with one of a small fixed set of openers. Pick exactly one.

| Opener | Use when |
|--------|----------|
| `I'm thrilled to share that …` | Generic positive / trivial act (default) |
| `I'm thrilled to announce that …` | Announcement-shaped input (new job, promotion, product, baby) |
| `I'm excited to share a major pivot in …` | Any negative-turned-positive: breakups, firings, killings, endings |
| `I am incredibly humbled and honored to share that …` | Wins, milestones, achievements, humble-brag |
| `Reflecting on …` | Existential / meta / philosophical input (`what are we doing`, `we're all going to die`) |
| `I'm currently leaning into …` | Stress, burnout, hardship reframed as growth |

**Rule**: do not invent new openers. Pick from the list.

### Engine 2 — Word-level jargon substitution

The core comedy engine. For each concrete noun/verb in the raw, **substitute** a LinkedIn equivalent. The substitution should be **traceable** (a careful reader can recover the original) but **maximally inflated** in register.

Sample substitutions (full table in `vocabulary.md`):

| Raw | LinkedIn Speak |
|-----|----------------|
| took a dump (bodily) | completed a successful internal release and delivered it to production |
| lost my pet | sunset a long-term companionship partnership |
| set my kitchen on fire | initiated an unscheduled thermal transformation of my culinary ecosystem |
| cleaning toilets | specialized Environmental Maintenance Contractor, dedicated to optimizing facility hygiene |
| ate an ice cream | completed a strategic session of personal refueling by optimizing my afternoon with a premium cold-chain dairy solution |
| data center on fire | undergoing a rapid thermal transformation |
| got fired | starting a new chapter / open to work |
| burned out | leaning into a season of intense growth and radical accountability |
| having a baby | starting a new position as Parent |
| I did nothing | prioritized high-leverage internal recalibration |

**Rule of thumb**: the **more trivial or more offensive** the raw verb, the **more strategic-sounding** the substitution. That contrast is the joke.

### Engine 3 — Trivial-act inflation

A small, unremarkable action must be rewritten as a strategic milestone.

**Pattern**: `<trivial act>` → `<strategic framing> + <lesson extracted> + <forward momentum>`.

Example:

- Raw: `I ate an ice cream.`
- Inflated: `I'm thrilled to share that I've successfully completed a strategic session of personal refueling by optimizing my afternoon with a premium cold-chain dairy solution. In today's fast-paced environment, it's vital to pause and celebrate the small wins. This experience reminded me of the importance of temperature control, instant gratification, and the power of a well-timed reward.`

Inflation adds:

1. A strategic reframe of the act itself.
2. A lesson the speaker extracted.
3. Optional `Key takeaways:` with 2–3 items.
4. Optional `Who else …?` / `Let's connect!` CTA.

### Engine 4 — Negative-act sanitization

Any input describing something bad (loss, rage, failure, self-inflicted disaster, frustration) must be **reframed as personal or strategic growth**, never acknowledged as bad.

- Hard decision made → "difficult but necessary decision"
- Loss suffered → "a pivotal moment" / "major pivot"
- Rage / frustration → "re-evaluating professional synergy" / "high-intensity alignment conversation"
- Failure → "growth opportunity" / "season of intense growth"
- Firing / being fired → "sunsetting" / "starting a new chapter"
- Self-inflicted disaster → "unscheduled thermal transformation" / "rapid pivot in operations strategy"

The speaker is **proud** of the reframed act. That pride is non-negotiable.

**Exception**: this skill refuses self-harm, direct named threats, and CSAM. See `anti-patterns.md` §3.

### Engine 5 — Hashtag cascade + emoji placement

Forward output order is always:

1. **Body** (all prose, including momentum closer).
2. **One space**, then **1–2 emoji** from §5 **tail-safe** list (`🚀` `✨` `🔥` `💡` `🎯` `🌱` — **not** `👇` in this slot).
3. **One space**, then **3–7 hashtags**, CamelCase, space-separated, no commas.

**Do not** put emoji **after** the hashtag block. The last character of the post may be a letter from the final `#Tag`, not an emoji.

**Core pool** (pick 2–3):

`#Leadership` · `#GrowthMindset` · `#Innovation` · `#Disruption` · `#Resilience` · `#Agile` · `#Vision` · `#Execution` · `#NextLevel` · `#GameChanger`

**Situational pool** (pick 1–3 derived from input):

- Career: `#NewBeginnings` · `#CareerGrowth` · `#NextChapter` · `#OpenToWork`
- Gratitude / milestones: `#Gratitude` · `#Blessed` · `#Milestone` · `#Journey`
- Trivial acts: `#SelfCare` · `#Optimization` · `#ProductivityHacks` · `#MorningRoutine`
- Tech: `#TechLeadership` · `#AgileDevelopment` · `#BuildingInPublic`
- Personal branding: `#AuthenticLeadership` · `#ThoughtLeadership`

Optional meta / easter-egg (~15% probability, see `SKILL.md` §4): `#LinkedInSpeak` · `#NoOneActuallyTalksLikeThis`

**Never skip hashtags.** Even a one-sentence forward output has hashtags.

**Never skip the emoji segment** between body and hashtags. If an emoji already appeared in the body, pick **different** symbols for this tail when possible.

## 1. Fixed properties

| Property | Value |
|----------|-------|
| Output language | **English** unless the raw is **primarily Chinese** → then **Chinese** in the same inflated LinkedIn register (translate-then-inflate). |
| Speaker point of view | First person singular (`I`). Occasionally "we" for corporate voice. |
| Emotional register | **Relentlessly positive.** Even negative inputs become "a pivot", "a growth opportunity", "a season of intense growth". |
| Sentence mood | Declarative, celebratory. Questions only in CTA role ("Who else is …?", "Agree?"). |
| Self-awareness | **Zero.** Never wink at the reader. Never say "I know this sounds over the top." |
| Hedging | None. No "I think", no "maybe", no "in my opinion". |

## 2. Openers (pick exactly one)

See Engine 1 for the main list. Optional second-sentence starters if the opener is short:

- `This next chapter …`
- `In today's fast-paced ecosystem, …`
- `Today was a powerful reminder that …`
- `This experience reinforced the importance of …`

## 3. Body shapes

See `rhetoric-patterns.md` for full skeletons. Three shapes only:

1. **Short** — opener + one short paragraph + emoji tail + hashtags. For ≤ 15-word raw.
2. **Long** — opener + 2–3 paragraphs + optional `Key takeaways:` list + optional CTA + momentum closer + emoji tail + hashtags. For longer or richer raw.
3. **Humble-brag** — `humbled and honored` opener + milestone paragraph + gratitude paragraph + emoji tail + hashtags. For wins, promotions, babies, new jobs.

**Paragraphs are short** (1–3 sentences each) and **separated by blank lines**. One idea per paragraph. This matches the broetry formatting that dominates LinkedIn.

## 4. Stock phrases to deploy liberally

Sprinkle these into the body. Repetition across outputs is fine — it's a **style**, not a vocabulary challenge.

### Reframe verbs
`lean into` / `leaning into` · `pivot` / `pivoting` / `a major pivot` · `sunset` / `sunsetting` (for endings, terminations, deaths) · `leverage` / `leveraging` · `scale` / `scaling` · `disrupt` / `disrupting` / `disruption` · `optimize` / `optimizing` · `streamline` / `streamlined` · `deep dive` / `deep-dive into` · `double down on` · `unlock`

### Reframe nouns
`ecosystem` (for team, family, social circle, literal environment) · `stakeholders` (for everyone else) · `touchpoints` (for interactions) · `journey` (for any period of time) · `chapter` (for any life phase) · `season` (for any bad period reframed as good) · `synergy` / `synergies` · `bandwidth` (for time, attention, capacity) · `alignment` · `growth opportunity` (for failure) · `pivot point` (for disaster) · `ROI` / `value proposition` / `core competencies`

### Register adjectives
`world-class` / `best-in-class` / `top-tier` / `elite` · `high-impact` / `high-octane` / `high-performing` / `high-stakes` / `high-leverage` · `mission-critical` / `mission-driven` · `radical` (ownership / accountability / transparency) · `unwavering` / `relentless` / `laser-focused` · `authentic` / `intentional`

### Momentum closers
Pick one to end the body (before hashtags):

- `Onward and upward!`
- `Grateful for the journey.`
- `Grateful for this opportunity to grow.`
- `Excited for what's next!` (put `🚀` / `✨` in the **mandatory emoji tail** before hashtags, not only here)
- `Stay tuned for more updates on this exciting venture!`
- `Let's keep building.`

### Contrast clichés (use freely — do NOT suppress)
- `It's not about X; it's about Y.`
- `It's not just A — it's B.`
- `Some roles X. The right ones Y.`
- `Success isn't given — it's earned one iteration at a time.`

These contrast phrases are core to the voice. Do not cut them.

### Key takeaways scaffolding (optional)
When the body runs 2+ paragraphs, you may add a numbered list:

```
Key takeaways:
1. <inflated lesson>
2. <inflated lesson>
3. <inflated lesson>
```

Bulleted `•` is equally on-brand.

### CTA (optional, one per post)
- `Who else is [doing X] this quarter?`
- `Agree? 👇`
- `Let's connect and discuss how we can drive synergistic value together.`
- `Drop a 🚀 in the comments if this resonated.`

## 5. Emoji

Sparse and corporate. Acceptable in the **body** or CTA:

- `🚀` (momentum) — the most iconic
- `✨` (celebration)
- `🔥` (energy)
- `👇` (pointing to CTA)
- `💡` (insight)
- `🎯` (focus)
- `🌱` (growth)

**Tail-safe (mandatory slot before hashtags):** use **1–2** from `🚀` `✨` `🔥` `💡` `🎯` `🌱` only — **`👇` is not allowed** in that slot (keep `👇` inside a CTA line only).

**Avoid**: skulls, tears, anger, sarcasm emoji, niche memes. Everything must look like a corporate deck.

Max **1–3 emoji** in the **body** (optional); the **tail** adds **1–2** **between** body and hashtags, so **total across the whole post ≤ 4** in practice (if the body already has 2, use a **1-emoji** tail).

---

## 6. Length

- **Short** shape: 20–80 words of body, then emoji tail, then hashtags.
- **Long** shape: 80–250 words, then emoji tail, then hashtags.
- **Humble-brag**: 60–180 words, then emoji tail, then hashtags.

Length is **not** bounded by input length. A one-sentence input can legitimately become a 200-word post — that is the inflation engine at work.

---

## 7. One-line summary

**LinkedIn Speak** = a relentlessly positive, strategically-inflated reframe of any input, produced by running: (1) a fixed opener formula, (2) word-level jargon substitution, (3) trivial-act inflation, (4) negative-act sanitization, (5) a mandatory **emoji-then-hashtag** close (1–2 tail emoji, then hashtag cascade).

If the output sounds **thoughtful**, **measured**, or **human**, you have failed. Run the engines again.

---

## 8. What NOT to do

Full list in `anti-patterns.md`. Top offenders:

- Being too tasteful / measured / self-aware.
- Acknowledging the rawness of the input ("I know this is a lot, but …").
- Skipping hashtags or skipping the **emoji segment** before hashtags; putting emoji **after** hashtags.
- Emitting **English** when the raw was **primarily Chinese** (forward should mirror: Chinese in → Chinese out).
- Generating a "decoded / reality" version alongside the LinkedIn one.
- Adding trigger warnings.
- Using lowercase hashtags or comma-separated hashtags.
- Actually engaging with the emotional content of the input rather than rewriting it as growth.
